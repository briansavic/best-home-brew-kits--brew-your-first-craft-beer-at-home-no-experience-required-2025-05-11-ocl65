Typical Inputs for Your Brew

When preparing to brew beer, the typical inputs for the fields in your calculator can vary based on the specific recipe and style of beer you are aiming to produce. However, here are some general guidelines for typical inputs:

1. Batch Size (liters):

  • Homebrewers often brew in batch sizes ranging from 5 liters to 20 liters. A common batch size for beginners is around 10 liters. This allows for manageable quantities and is suitable for experimenting with different recipes.

2. Malt Extract (kg per liter):

  • The amount of malt extract used can vary depending on the desired alcohol content and flavor profile. A typical range is between 0.1 kg to 0.2 kg of malt extract per liter of water. For example, a light beer might use around 0.1 kg/L, while a stronger ale might use up to 0.2 kg/L or more.

3. Hops (grams per liter):

  • The amount of hops used depends on the bitterness, flavor, and aroma desired in the beer. A typical range is between 1 gram to 5 grams of hops per liter. For example, a light lager might use around 1-2 grams/L, while a more bitter IPA might use 3-5 grams/L or more.

Example Inputs:

  • Batch Size: 10 liters
  • Malt Extract: 0.15 kg/L
  • Hops: 3 grams/L

These values are just starting points and can be adjusted based on personal preference and the specific style of beer you are brewing. It's always a good idea to consult a detailed recipe or brewing guide for more precise measurements tailored to the type of beer you want to make.
